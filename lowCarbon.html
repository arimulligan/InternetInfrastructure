This is a Low Carbon Website!

Welcome to our low carbon website, where sustainability meets seamless design for a greener and more efficient online experience.

Why Low Carbon?


A low carbon web design approach is poised to become increasingly important in the design profession in the near future. As sustainability concerns continue to grow, designers will be expected to take a more environmentally responsible approach to their work. Low carbon web design not only aligns with these emerging environmental values but also addresses the practicality of creating faster, more efficient, and accessible websites.

Moreover, as regulatory and consumer demands for eco-conscious practices rise, designers who embrace low carbon principles will likely have a competitive edge in the marketplace. Therefore, it is my opinion that low carbon web design is not just a sustainable choice but also a strategic one for the design profession as it navigates the challenges of a changing world.

Low Carbon Techniques Used

Technique Why?
Dithered Images The use of dithered images is beneficial for low carbon web design because it helps reduce the file size of images while maintaining visual quality, ultimately leading to lower energy consumption and a smaller carbon footprint. Dithering is a technique that adds noise or patterns to images, allowing for the representation of more colors and details with fewer pixels. This results in smaller image files, which require less bandwidth and storage space, thereby decreasing the data transfer and server energy consumption associated with serving images to users. In a world where image-heavy websites can be resource-intensive, dithering contributes to a more energy-efficient and environmentally responsible web design approach.

I used a color palette size of 6 using the Floyd Steinberg dithering method.
Default Typeface / No Logo Utilizing default typefaces and forgoing logos in low carbon web design is a strategic choice for a more sustainable and efficient online presence. These design decisions prioritize reduced load times and minimal bandwidth usage by relying on web-safe fonts already available on users' devices, resulting in faster page loading and decreased energy consumption associated with data transfer. The simplicity and clarity of such a design enhance the overall user experience while reducing maintenance overhead. This approach not only demonstrates environmental responsibility but also ensures cross-device compatibility, aligning perfectly with the principles of a greener and more accessible web.

I did this by deleting the whole package of web fonts that was in this HTML5Up template, and deleting all occurances of 'font-family'. I then deleted all the favicons and the links in the headers for each of these things.
No Third-Party Tracking, No Advertising Services, No Cookies A web design approach that excludes third-party tracking, advertising services, and cookies offers numerous benefits: including enhanced privacy, faster loading times, simplified user experience, lower resource consumption, improved security, compliance with privacy regulations, and sustainability. By avoiding these elements, websites can foster trust, reduce data breaches, and improve user experience. This approach also reduces energy consumption and operational costs, ensuring a more predictable and secure browsing experience. Furthermore, it contributes to a more sustainable and environmentally responsible internet, reducing energy consumption for data transmission.

I did this by not letting any third parties put advertising, or install any plugins for the website which could track their movements. I have no cookies in this website because the website does not need to know who the user is.
Dark Coloured Design Because the more dark you go, the less energy the computer will have to use for brightness, so a more sustainable outcome for each of the users.
